# Uniqueness in Poincare's theorem[^1]
Assume that $\operatorname{Aut}_{K}(A)$ is a finite group. 

Prove that the decomposition in [[lombardo_av_THEOREM 7.1|Poincar√©'s theorem]] is unique[^2] (that is, there exist unique [[lombardo_av_DEFINITION 5.18#Simple abelian variety|simple abelian subvarieties]] $A_{1}, \ldots, A_{n}$ of $A$ such that the sum is an [[lombardo_av_DEFINITION 5.3|isogeny]] $A_{1} \times$ $\left.\cdots \times A_{n} \rightarrow A\right)$. 

[^2]: Up to isogeny

Show with an example that uniqueness of the decomposition does not hold in general.
